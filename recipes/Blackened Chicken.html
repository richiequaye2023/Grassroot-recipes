Blackened Chicken

Blackened chicken is great on a summer's day with fresh corn and mashed potatoes. Food that is blackened isn't burnt; it's simply coated in a special spice blend that takes on a very dark brown, almost black color when cooked in a skillet, on a grill or in an oven. Blackened food almost always means blackened meat, chicken or seafood, including fish and shellfish like shrimp. Blackening spices are a blend that uses traditional herbs and spices from both Cajun and Creole cooking.

  • ⅛ teaspoon salt
